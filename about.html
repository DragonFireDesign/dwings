DEATHWINGS // ISSUE 01

EDITOR'S NOTE

THE EXPIRATION
DATE IS A LIE.

They'll tell you passion has a shelf life. That at some point you're supposed to put the board away, quiet the music, trade intensity for comfort.

Society doesn't just suggest you slow down — it expects it. Corporate culture. Social norms. Even well-meaning advice all push the same message: grow up, settle in, stop chasing what lights you up.

YOUTH ISN'T A
BIRTHDATE.
IT'S A COMBAT SPORT.

What they don't tell you is this: The best work doesn't come early. It comes from time spent. From repetition. From refusal. Real mastery isn't youthful — it's earned.

DEATHWINGS exists for the people who never accepted the timeline. The ones who kept going when they were told to stop. Who didn't "outgrow" what gave them life — they deepened it.

This isn't nostalgia. It isn't rebellion for show.

It's proof that intensity doesn't expire — it evolves.

— The Editors, Deathwings
